<p><p>The Digital Photosensitive Material market primarily consists of two types of sensor technologies: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) chips. CCD chips are known for their high image quality and sensitivity, making them ideal for professional photography and scientific applications. In contrast, CMOS chips offer lower power consumption, faster processing speeds, and integration capabilities, making them popular in consumer electronics like smartphones and webcams. Both technologies continue to evolve, catering to various imaging needs across different sectors.</p></p>
